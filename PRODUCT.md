# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Legal professionals — law firm associates, in-house counsel, and arbitrators — conducting cross-jurisdictional research across DIFC and ADGM legal systems. They upload court judgments, arbitration rulings, and regulatory circulars as PDFs, then query them for cited answers. Under time pressure, need precision and trustworthiness.

## Product Purpose

DIFC Lex is a legal precedent navigator. It ingests DIFC court judgments, ADGM arbitration rulings, UAE federal laws, English common law precedents, and DFSA/FSRA regulatory circulars. Users ask natural-language questions and receive answers with specific citations and quoted passages from source documents. The core value is cross-jurisdictional comparison — DIFC and ADGM are dual financial free zones with distinct legal systems, and comparing them is a real pain point for law firms.

## Brand Personality

Calm, clear, trustworthy. Like a well-organized law library. Confident but not cold. Every element earns its place. No decoration, no distraction. The interface recedes so the content — legal reasoning and citations — commands attention.

## Anti-references

- **No flashy SaaS clichés**: no neon accents, no gradient text, no glassmorphism, no hero-metric templates. Nothing that looks like a crypto dashboard or a startup landing page.
- **No legacy legal database feel**: no dense data tables, no tiny text, no 90s enterprise gray. Nothing that feels like Westlaw or LexisNexis.

## Design Principles

1. **Citations are the product.** Every design decision serves the answer-and-citation flow. The reading experience is paramount.
2. **Recede to reveal.** The interface is quiet so the content speaks. Typography and spacing do the work; chrome stays minimal.
3. **Trust through restraint.** No decorative elements. Precision in layout, typography, and spacing signals reliability.
4. **One thing at a time.** Upload, preview, query — each action has clear focus. The 3-pane layout supports the workflow without competing for attention.
5. **Session-only, no trace.** Nothing persists. The interface communicates this transience through its lightness.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG 2.1 AA minimum
- All body text ≥4.5:1 contrast against background
- Keyboard-navigable: all interactive elements reachable via Tab
- Focus indicators visible on all interactive elements
- Screen reader support for dynamic content (chat messages, PDF loading states)
- Respects `prefers-reduced-motion`
